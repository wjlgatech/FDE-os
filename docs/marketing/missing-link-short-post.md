You can build an AI portfolio in 60 seconds.

That's exactly why it won't get you hired.

Hiring leads check three things: can you build, can you learn fast, and will you actually show up and deliver.

A portfolio can *show* the first two — real repos, running demos. Evidence, not claims. Good.

The third one — reliability — it structurally cannot prove. Because you can't vouch for yourself. A page you wrote about you is not a reference.

And when everyone spins one up in a minute, a self-narrated portfolio stops being a signal at all. It slides back into a prettier résumé.

So I built a gate to test it. Fed it a strong portfolio: clean code, green builds on every commit. It returned NO-GO — not missing code, missing a *person*. Zero vouches.

Then one former teammate signed a 30-second structured reference: worked together, would staff again, never went dark. The same portfolio flipped to GO.

A green build couldn't do it. One honest human could.

Call it the attestation gap — the space between what you can say about yourself and what someone else will stake their name on. Everyone's racing to fill the tier below it.

The résumé died because anyone could write anything. Let's not rebuild it with nicer fonts.

What's the strongest signal you've ever seen that someone would truly deliver?
