The first résumé in history was written in 1482, and it was already a pile of unverified claims.

Leonardo da Vinci told the Duke of Milan: "I can dry up the water of the moats." Moats dried at time of writing: roughly zero. What made us believe him, five centuries later, wasn't the letter — it was 7,000 pages of notebooks.

544 years later, most interview prep is still letter-writing: cram the docs, rehearse adjectives about yourself.

Here's the notebook approach, updated. A job description is source code:

1. Parse it — extract what the role actually demands, deterministically.
2. Ground it — every concept resolves to a real definition with a source URL, or gets flagged. Never faked.
3. Link it — resolve each requirement against your public repos, like a linker resolving symbols.
4. Read the errors — whatever fails to link IS your prep plan.

I ran this on a real senior agentic-AI posting. Most symbols resolved. One didn't: a framework the JD named twice and my repos knew nothing about.

One error message. One half-day artifact. Exit 0.

Cramming resets after every interview. Compiling accrues — each artifact you ship is already linked when the next JD compiles.
