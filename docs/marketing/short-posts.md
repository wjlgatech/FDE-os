## Post 2 — "the loop is the skill" (≈180 words)

Unpopular opinion: in 2026, prompting is not the skill. **The loop** is.

Here's the loop. You change one line. An agent runs the task. A scorer checks the output. Score goes
up, you keep it. Score drops, you revert it — instantly. Then it all commits to git, which quietly
becomes the memory of what made your AI better and what broke it.

A prompt you copy-paste every time is a chore. A versioned, evaluated, looping artifact runs your AI
every day without you babysitting it. That's the difference between doing a task and owning a system.

I'm building this into FDE-os — an open operating system for Forward Deployed Engineers, the role
every AI lab is now racing to hire. Seven tested tools, three quality gates, one self-improving loop.
It runs offline, and it scored *this post* before you read it. (It passed. Barely. I'm working on my
hooks.)
