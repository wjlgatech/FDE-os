# Delta — Community & Platform Strategy

*The systematic answer to "where do we build the FDE community to maximize exposure AND depth, and turn consumers into creators and contributors?"*

## The core principle: hub-and-spoke, not a single platform

A community has two opposing jobs:
- **Exposure** — wide, borrowed, shallow. Be where the fish already swim.
- **Depth** — narrow, owned, deep. One room where people actually bond.

No single platform does both. So: **rented spokes for exposure, one owned hub for depth.** Everything flows inward to the hub.

```
   LinkedIn ──┐
   Hacker News ─┤
   Existing Discords ─┤──►  CAPTURE  ──►   THE HUB
   Palantir Alumni ─┤      (email list)    (owned relationship:
   Reddit ──┘                              email list + Delta chat)
  SPOKES (rented, exposure)   the ONE         where consumers become
                              owned asset      creators & contributors
```

## The hard rule: own the list, rent everything else, build nothing custom yet

The only thing you truly own is the **email list**. Algorithms change, platforms die, but a list is portable. A bespoke community website/forum is premature optimization — an empty house you have to heat. The **landing page + email list + Discord IS the owned hub.** Don't build more until the rooms are crowded.

## Why people get hooked: identity, not features

FDEs are **lonely** — embedded solo at customer sites, the only AI person in the room, no peers who get it. The hook is *"finally, my people."* The upward engine is **status**: contribute → get recognized → become known in the niche.

## The participation ladder (consumer → creator → contributor)

90-9-1 reality: most lurk, few engage, very few create. Design explicit rungs + prompts + rewards to pull people up:

| Rung | Action | Mechanism / prompt | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Consumer | Reads an essay | LinkedIn/HN post | Insight |
| 2. Subscriber | Gives email | Landing page CTA | (the owned conversion) |
| 3. Member | Joins the chat | Welcome email → invite | Belonging |
| 4. Creator | Shares one 2 a.m. war story | Templated, low-effort ritual (= Post #10) | Recognition |
| 5. Contributor | Ships to toolkit / teaches a module | "Good first issue" / micro-module slot | Status + identity |

## The compounding loop

**Member war stories → become Delta essays (with credit) → exposure on the spokes → pull in new members.**

The community feeds the content engine. Creator gets public credit (status), Delta gets content (fuel), the niche gets a home (moat). The community and the blog are the *same flywheel*, not two projects.

## Recommended sequence (don't do it all at once)

1. **Now:** Email list live (wire the landing page to a provider). Stand up the Delta Discord with a minimal structure + warm welcome message. Create r/ForwardDeployed (flag-plant).
2. **Next:** Make LinkedIn the primary spoke — publish Post #1 natively + as a LinkedIn Newsletter; route to the landing page.
3. **Then:** Seed 2-3 existing communities (HN + Palantir Alumni + one applied-AI Discord) by adding value, not advertising.
4. **Only after flow exists:** consider an owned website/forum.

**Guardrail:** every new surface must route to the owned hub (email). If it doesn't capture, it doesn't count.
